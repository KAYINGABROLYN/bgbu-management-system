About The Boys' and Girls' Brigade of Uganda

Our History

The Boys' and Girls' Brigade of Uganda (BGBU) was established in [YEAR] as a Christian youth organization operating under the Church of Uganda but open to members of all faiths. Our mission has always been to nurture young people into responsible, empowered, and Christ-centered leaders.

Over the years, we have grown from a small local group to a national organization with branches across Uganda, impacting thousands of young lives annually.

Our Vision

To nurture boys and girls into responsible, empowered, and Christ-centered leaders in service to God and society.

Our Mission

To promote the spiritual, physical, and moral development of young people through structured Christian programs, leadership training, and community service.

Our Objectives

  • The advancement of Christ's Kingdom and the promotion of Christian character
  • Promoting Christian principles among boys and girls
  • Developing leadership qualities, discipline, and responsibility
  • Fostering personal growth through various activities
  • Providing opportunities for community service
  • Promoting unity, friendship, and collaboration among youth
  • Offering a safe and supportive environment for growth

Our Structure

BGBU is organized at national, regional, district (battalion), and local (company) levels to ensure effective administration and program delivery.

National Council

Supreme decision-making body

Regional Councils

Coordinate activities in their regions

Battalion Councils

Oversee district-level operations

Company Units

Local implementation units
